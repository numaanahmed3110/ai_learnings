# AI Engineer Glossary

The canonical language for this workspace. Every lesson uses these terms.

> Terms are promoted here **only once you can use them correctly** — the
> glossary is a record of compressed understanding, not a word list to memorise.
> It grows lesson by lesson.

## The landscape

**Artificial Intelligence (AI)**:
Any technique that makes a machine perform tasks that seem to require human
intelligence — from hand-written rules to learned models.

**Machine Learning (ML)**:
The subset of AI where a system *learns* patterns from data instead of being
given explicit rules.
_Avoid_: "the algorithm figured it out"

**Deep Learning**:
The subset of ML that uses many-layered neural networks, which discover useful
features from data on their own.

**Generative AI**:
Deep learning that *produces* new content (text, images, audio, code) rather
than only classifying or predicting a label.

**Large Language Model (LLM)**:
The text branch of generative AI — a model that generates language. In this
workspace, "the model" means an LLM unless stated otherwise.
_Avoid_: "the AI", "ChatGPT" (that's one product built on an LLM)

## Levels

**Narrow AI (ANI)**:
An AI competent at one task or a narrow band of tasks. **Everything that exists
today is narrow**, including the most capable LLMs.
_Avoid_: "weak AI"

**Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)**:
Hypothetical AI with human-level competence across *any* task. Does not exist
today. Distinct from — and not implied by — generative ability.

## Roles

**AI Engineer**:
Builds products on top of *existing, pretrained* models (prompting, RAG, agents,
APIs). Software-heavy, math-light. **This is the workspace's target role.**

**ML Engineer**:
Trains, deploys, and optimises *custom* models; owns pipelines and MLOps. Carries
a heavier math/stats load than the AI Engineer.

**AI Researcher**:
Invents *new* models, architectures, and methods; math-heavy; output is new
knowledge (papers).
